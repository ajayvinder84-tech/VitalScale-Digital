What's happening right now

A family searches for NDIS support in your area. Here's what usually happens.

This plays out dozens of times every week in most suburbs — and most providers never know it's happening.

1

They open Google

A parent or support coordinator types "NDIS provider [suburb]" or "disability support near me." They expect to find the best local options.

2

They check the star rating, scan for reviews, and look at the website. If anything feels unclear or unestablished, they move on immediately. They rarely scroll further.

Not because that provider is better. Simply because their Google profile looked more trusted — more reviews, clearer info, a website that loaded fast and made sense.

4

What we see every day

The gaps that are quietly costing you enquiries

These aren't failures — they're common issues that build up when you're focused on doing the actual work of supporting people.

No reviews, or very few

Families use reviews as a trust signal. A provider with no reviews looks unproven — even when they've been delivering excellent care for years.

A website that loses visitors

If your site is slow, confusing, or doesn't clearly explain your services and how to get in touch — visitors leave in seconds without ever reaching out.

Not appearing in local search

If families search your suburb and you don't appear in the top results, you simply don't exist to them. Local visibility is where almost every new enquiry begins.

No consistent source of new enquiries

Relying entirely on word-of-mouth or coordinator referrals works — until it doesn't. Without a visible online presence, your pipeline is always at someone else's mercy.

An incomplete Google Business profile

An incomplete or unclaimed Google profile is one of the most common issues we find. It directly affects where you appear in search and how credible you look to families.

Unclear service and trust signals

If a visitor can't quickly understand what you offer, who you support, and why you're trustworthy — they won't reach out. Clarity builds confidence before a single word is spoken.
