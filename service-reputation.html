Reputation & Review Strategy

Reviews are the first thing families and coordinators check. We help you build a genuine review presence that reflects the quality of your care.

For NDIS providers, online reviews carry particular weight. Families making decisions about disability support are often in emotionally difficult situations — and a provider with no reviews, or with unanswered negative reviews, triggers doubt at exactly the moment trust is most important.

We help you build a genuine, consistent review presence — in a way that respects participant privacy and dignity, and is appropriate for the NDIS sector. We also set up monitoring and response systems so your reputation is actively managed over time. A well-managed review presence doesn't just attract families — it also signals quality to support coordinators who regularly check provider profiles before making referrals.

What's included

  • Current review profile audit across Google, Clickability, and key directories
  • Ethical review generation strategy appropriate for NDIS providers
  • Review request templates for staff, partner organisations, and coordinators
  • Google review response framework with tone and messaging guide
  • Process for managing negative or inaccurate reviews professionally
  • Reputation monitoring setup with regular alerts
  • Guidance on participant story and testimonial capture (consent-focused)
  • Quarterly review presence reporting

Common questions

Can we ask participants to leave reviews?

Yes, with care. We help you develop an approach that is consensual, participant-led, and appropriate for the NDIS context. We never recommend incentivising reviews or pressuring participants — both for ethical reasons and because it violates platform terms.

What about negative reviews — can they be removed?

Genuine negative reviews generally cannot be removed from Google. What we help you do is respond to them professionally — which research shows can actually improve overall perception — and build a consistent positive review base that contextualises any outliers.

How long does it take to build a meaningful review presence?

With a consistent strategy, most providers see meaningful improvement within 2–3 months. Building to 20+ reviews typically takes 4–6 months depending on your participant volume and outreach approach.

Is it appropriate for NDIS providers to seek reviews?

Yes — when done ethically. Many NDIS providers avoid asking for reviews out of discomfort, which leaves their online reputation unmanaged. We help you develop a professional, appropriate approach that respects participant dignity and complies with NDIS practice standards.
